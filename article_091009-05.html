09.10.2009 Our girls go down fighting

by Chen Hui Jing

As Head Coach Tan See Wah mentioned, there will always be the pressure of expectation when playing in front of a home crowd. Perhaps that was what plagued our girls tonight.

Kew Suik May opened the match with a 3-pointer, but as the game went on, it was evident that it was not a good night for the Malaysian shooters. Both teams started off tight and shots from both teams refused to fall. The Chinese, with their size advantage over our girls pounded the ball inside and managed to score in the paint. The first quarter ended with a score of 7-14.

Our girls tightened their defense in the 2nd quarter, scoring 4 in a row off of Chinese turnovers. However, the Chinese had an obvious size advantage inside and managed to score off second shot opportunities.

The taller Chinese team made it hard for our girls to penetrate the inside and with our shooters cold from the outside, the Chinese pushed their lead to 10 at the six-minute mark. The Guangzhou girls found their rhythm and started hitting shots from the perimeter as well.

Our girls, though smaller in size, were relentless in their pursuit of the ball and managed to outrebound the much taller Chinese 20-17 in the first half. However, our girls did not shoot the ball well during the first half, hitting only a dismal 23% from the field.

The Chinese, on the other hand, found their groove towards the end of the second quarter, shooting 40% from the field and extending their lead to 16 going into the halftime break.

The Malaysians continued with the tenacious defense and in the second half and started hitting from the outside, with Kew Suik May, Goh Beng Fong and Ang Siew Teng scoring from beyond the arc. Our girls went on a 16-5 run and managed to cut the lead to 10 by the two-minute mark of the third quarter.

However, our girls were unable to find their shooting touch and even though they outworked their opponents on defense and fought hard for every rebound, their meager shooting percentage allowed the Chinese to maintain a double-digit lead throughout the second half.

Kew Suik May hit two 3-pointers near the end of the fourth quarter but it was too little too late and the game ended with a final score of 54-66. A glaring statistic on the scoresheet was points in the paint, in which the Chinese outscored our girls 42-16. The Malaysians only managed 28% from the field while the Chinese made 46% of their shots.

Three Guangzhou players scored in double figures, Yang Li Wei and Qiu Si Yue both had 19 points apiece. While center, Yu Dong, had a double-double with 14 points and 11 rebounds.

Kew Suik May was the only Malaysian player to find her shooting touch, making 6-of-13 shots from beyond the arc, ending the night with 20 points. Ang Siew Teng and Pee Yann Yann scored 9 points and 8 points respectively.

 

 

 

 

 
 

（吉隆坡9日讯）马来西亚娘子军拉不响首炮，以54比66不敌中国广州青年队。 虽然刚在印度拿下亚洲女篮赛乙级冠军，但回到自家门前作战的马来西亚队未能抵住主场压力，尽管篮板球（37比35）还可与对手一拼，然投篮命中率在对手的严防下偏低，仅得22%，反观广州青年队在国青邱思月独取19分的带领下，取得46%的命中率。

邱淑媚和许淑殷的3分球为马来西亚拉开战幔，惟失球率偏高加上防守欠积极， 让广州青年队以14比7领先首节。 马来西亚次节加强防守后略见起色，但却难阻中国国青邱思月和国家少年队杨力维的强势进攻，两人合取25分，助中国以34比18结束上半场。

主队在下半场开端3分球告急，加上广州青年队密不透风的防线，多次出手落空，幸好彭燕燕多次偷袭成功， 配合吴明凤和邱淑媚的适时3分球，才从21分最大落差逼近30比43。 淑媚在末节球风大起，3次射穿对手3分篮框，惜独木难撑，马来西亚最终以12分饮恨。

马来西亚仅淑媚得分达双，投进6个3分球贡献20分；广青的邱思月和杨力维各有19分进帐，195公分的高妹于东添14分。
